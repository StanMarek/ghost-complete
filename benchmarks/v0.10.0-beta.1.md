# Benchmarks — v0.10.0-beta.1

Date: 2026-04-25
Platform: macOS (Apple Silicon)

Criterion's reported `change` column compares against the previous saved run on this machine (v0.9.1 baseline).

The 0.10.0 cycle ships a large surface — eight new native async providers replacing JS-backed generators, build-time minification of embedded specs (`crates/gc-suggest/build.rs`), the `_corrected_in` lifecycle marker, dotted-path `json_extract`/`json_extract_array`, and a `suffix` transform. The most performance-relevant change is build-time minification: it strips `js_source` from every embedded generator and removes whitespace, shrinking the parsed payload that `spec_loading` measures. Hot per-keystroke paths (`fuzzy_ranking`, `transform_pipeline`, `engine_suggest_sync`) see no algorithmic change — expected delta is flat there, with a measurable improvement on `spec_loading`.

## Analysis

Every tracked group is flat or slightly faster than v0.9.1. The largest movement is `engine_suggest_sync/filesystem_fallback` at −6.1%, which is within typical noise for that benchmark (cold async filesystem walks vary run-to-run). The most semantically meaningful delta is `spec_loading/load_717_specs` at −2.6%: build-time embedded-spec minification (`gc-suggest/build.rs`) strips `js_source` and whitespace from the baked-in JSON, so the parser does less work at startup. Binary size dropped from ~47 MB to ~28.42 MB as the documented benefit; the parse-time win is the unbudgeted bonus.

No regression in the per-keystroke hot path: `fuzzy_ranking`, `transform_pipeline`, and `engine_suggest_sync/{command_position,subcommand_with_spec}` are all within ±2%.
