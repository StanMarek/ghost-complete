## Analysis

Every group is within ±5% of v0.10.0-beta.1, with the largest mover (`vt_parse_throughput/cursor_heavy` at +4.7%) sitting inside typical Criterion noise for that benchmark — the parser path saw no functional changes between beta.1 and stable, so the move is attributable to run-to-run variance on a busy laptop rather than code regression.

The new `priority_sort` group lands at 713 µs (empty query) and 634 µs (fuzzy query) on a 10k-candidate mixed-kind pool with explicit priority overrides every 50th item. Both sit comfortably under the `<1 ms / 10k-candidate` target documented in `CLAUDE.md`, confirming that the new `(history_bucket, score, priority, alpha)` sort tuple does not regress the hot-path budget.

Hot per-keystroke paths — `fuzzy_ranking/{1k_3char, 10k_3char, 10k_empty}`, `transform_pipeline/*`, `engine_suggest_sync/{command_position, subcommand_with_spec}` — are all within ±3%, and the keystroke-to-suggestion target of <50 ms (stretch <20 ms) remains intact: a typical end-to-end trigger walks at most one of each (parse → resolve → fuzzy-rank → engine dispatch) plus the new priority sort, summing to ~120-200 µs of synchronous work per keystroke.
